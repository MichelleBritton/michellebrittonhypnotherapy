Stress

According to the UK's government agency, the Health and Safety Executive, stress affects one in five of the working population and is now the biggest cause of sickness in the UK.

Stress is the feeling of being under too much pressure whether it is at work, in relationships or just life in general. These pressures turn into stress when they exceed your ability to cope with them.

All kinds of situations can cause stress and individuals have different stress thresholds, what may be stressful to one person may not necessarily be stressful to another. Some may find a certain amount of stress is a motivating factor for them, helping them to perform better in situations such as public speaking or just working their way through their daily task list.

Unfortunately stress can affect you both physically and mentally. When we are feeling stressed the body produces stress hormones to prepare you for the fight or flight response, increasing your heart rate and blood pressure. If the stress is temporary then the stress hormone levels will return to normal but individuals who suffer with prolonged stress can start to develop symptoms of anxiety such as headaches and migraines, sleeping problems, anger problems, Irritable Bowel Syndrome, Obsessive Compulsive Disorder to name just a few.

Negative thinking can trigger the same response just as a real threat does and Solution Focused Hypnotherapy will help clients to replace unhelpful negative thinking patterns with more beneficial and positive thoughts thereby reducing their stress and anxiety levels enabling them to deal with situations in a different and more positive way.
